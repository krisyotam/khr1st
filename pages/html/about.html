About Me

Biography

I head several projects aimed at freeing individuals from the modern constraints of surveillance, control, and overreach. These include Free People and Offline, which focus on promoting autonomy, privacy, and self-sovereignty. Additionally, I work on various open-source initiatives with a goal of enabling others to reclaim control over their lives and digital presence.

Vision

My mission is to help liberate individuals from the modern systems that infringe upon their freedom, whether through surveillance, excessive data collection, or other forms of control. Everything I do revolves around this core vision: to empower others through open-source projects and provide resources that enable true autonomy. My work aligns with a commitment to decentralization, privacy, and creating sustainable ways to preserve and enhance human freedom in the digital age.

Values

The values I live by are rooted in naturalness, economy, decentralization, minimalism, purity, asceticism, and peace. I believe in the seamless integration of technology with life, where systems are simple, intuitive, and deeply customizable. My focus is on creating and supporting programs that are lightweight, extensible, and decentralized, giving individuals greater control and freedom over their personal and digital environments.

Interests

I have a deep interest in technology, spanning from old to modern hardware, including a fascination with keyboards, programming languages, competitive coding, and Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges. My exploration also includes open-source software, system architecture, and everything related to decentralization and autonomy.
